# Time, Energy and Mass in PRM

**Projection Reality Model — PRM**  
**A conceptual interpretation of time, energy and mass as aspects of relational structure**

## Purpose

This document explores how the Projection Reality Model interprets time, energy and mass as related aspects of structured relational reality.

This is not intended as a replacement for established physical definitions.  
It is a conceptual layer used inside PRM to investigate whether time, energy and mass can be understood through a common relational pattern.

## General View

In PRM, reality is approached as a structured field of distinguishable relations.

Within this framework, a stable relational configuration may be treated as a **WR** — a Working Relation.

A WR can:

- preserve identity,
- resist change,
- undergo transformation,
- interact with other WR structures,
- project itself into observable form.

From this perspective, time, energy and mass are not treated as completely separate concepts. They are interpreted as different aspects of how relational structures persist, change and resist transformation.

## 1. Time

In PRM, **time** is interpreted as the relational ordering of change.

Time is not treated here as an independent object. It is understood as a condition through which change can be distinguished, ordered and compared.

Without distinguishable change, time cannot be meaningfully accessed.

### Working formulation

Time is the ordering of possible or actual change within a relational structure.

### PRM interpretation

A WR does not simply “exist in time”.  
Rather, time appears as a way of ordering the possible and actual transformations of WR structures.

This means that time is closely connected with:

- distinction,
- sequence,
- transformation,
- persistence,
- comparison between states.

In this interpretation, time is not only a background in which things happen. It is also a relational map of change.

## 2. Energy

In PRM, **energy** is interpreted as the capacity of a relational structure to undergo, resist or cause transformation.

This is not physical energy in the strict scientific sense. It is a conceptual interpretation used inside the model.

Energy describes the possibility of change.

### Working formulation

Energy is the capacity of a relational structure to participate in transformation.

### PRM interpretation

A WR may contain a certain level of transformative potential.

This potential may express itself as:

- readiness for change,
- ability to cause change,
- ability to move from one state to another,
- ability to disturb or reorganize another structure,
- ability to overcome resistance.

In this sense, energy is related to the possibility of transition between relational states.

## 3. Mass

In PRM, **mass** is interpreted as a form of relational resistance.

This does not replace the physical concept of mass. It is a conceptual reading of mass-like behavior as the persistence of relational structure against change.

### Working formulation

Mass is the projection of stable relational resistance.

### PRM interpretation

A structure that strongly preserves its organization against change may be interpreted as having mass-like relational resistance.

In this view, mass is connected with:

- stability,
- persistence,
- resistance to transformation,
- structural continuity,
- inertia-like behavior.

A WR with high relational resistance does not change easily.  
Its identity is strongly preserved across attempted transformations.

## 4. Protomass

**Protomass** is a PRM term for the pre-physical or conceptual form of mass-like resistance inside a relational structure.

It does not mean physical mass directly.

It means that a WR has a stable relational organization that resists change strongly enough to behave as a persistent unit within the model.

### Working formulation

Protomass is the stable resistance of a WR before it is interpreted as physical mass.

### PRM interpretation

If a WR is understood as a stable structure of relations, then protomass describes the degree to which this structure preserves itself.

A WR with high protomass:

- remains identifiable,
- resists disruption,
- maintains internal organization,
- persists through transformation,
- behaves as a stable unit of projection.

Protomass therefore describes stability before physical interpretation.

## 5. Protoenergy

**Protoenergy** is a PRM term for the capacity of a WR to enter, produce or support change.

It does not mean physical energy directly.

It means that a relational structure has a level of possibility for transformation.

### Working formulation

Protoenergy is the capacity of a WR to participate in change.

### PRM interpretation

A WR with high protoenergy has a greater potential to transform, influence or reorganize relational states.

Protoenergy may be understood as related to:

- possibility of transition,
- openness to change,
- ability to disturb stability,
- ability to overcome resistance,
- capacity to generate new projections.

In this sense, protoenergy and protomass are conceptually linked.

Protomass expresses resistance to change.  
Protoenergy expresses capacity for change.

## 6. Relation Between Protomass and Protoenergy

In PRM, protomass and protoenergy are not isolated properties.

They describe two complementary aspects of WR structure:

- protomass: how strongly a WR preserves its organization,
- protoenergy: how strongly a WR can enter or produce transformation.

A highly stable WR may have strong protomass.  
A highly transformative WR may have strong protoenergy.

The relation between them can be understood as a tension between stability and change.

## 7. Time as a Map of Change

If protomass describes resistance and protoenergy describes transformative capacity, then time can be understood as the ordering of possible transitions between WR states.

This gives rise to the PRM intuition of a **map of time**.

A map of time is not a physical map.  
It is a conceptual structure describing how possible changes are ordered within a relational system.

### Working formulation

Time is the relational map of possible and actual transformation.

## 8. Unified PRM Pattern

The three concepts can be summarized as follows:

- **mass / protomass** — resistance and stability of relational structure,
- **energy / protoenergy** — capacity for relational transformation,
- **time** — ordering of relational change.

Together, they describe a single pattern:

> A WR persists through resistance, changes through transformative capacity, and becomes ordered through time.

## 9. Important Limitation

This document does not claim that PRM has replaced physics.

The concepts presented here are exploratory and conceptual.

They are intended to help formulate a relational language that may later be compared with physical, informational and philosophical models.

Any physical interpretation would require separate mathematical formalization and empirical grounding.

## Summary

In PRM:

- time is the ordering of change,
- energy is the capacity for transformation,
- mass is resistance to transformation,
- protomass is stable relational resistance before physical interpretation,
- protoenergy is transformative capacity before physical interpretation,
- WR is the relational structure in which these aspects can be analyzed.

The central idea is that time, energy and mass may be interpreted as connected aspects of relational stability and change.
