# Information and Change in PRM

**Projection Reality Model — PRM**  
**A conceptual interpretation of information as distinguishable relational change**

## Purpose

This document explains how the Projection Reality Model interprets information and change.

In PRM, information is not treated only as data stored in a medium or transmitted between systems. It is interpreted more generally as a distinguishable difference within a relational structure.

The central idea is simple:

> Information appears where change becomes distinguishable.

## General View

PRM approaches reality as a structured field of relations.

Within this field, something becomes identifiable only when a distinction can be made. A distinction creates the possibility of recognizing difference. When such difference changes, persists, propagates or transforms, it may become information.

Information is therefore connected with:

- distinction,
- change,
- relation,
- recognition,
- transmission,
- transformation,
- preservation of difference.

## 1. Change

In PRM, **change** is a transformation in relational structure.

Change does not have to mean physical movement. It may also mean a change of state, pattern, relation, configuration, meaning or possibility.

A change becomes meaningful only when it can be distinguished from another state.

### Working formulation

Change is a distinguishable transformation in a relational structure.

## 2. Distinguishable Change

Not every transformation is information.

For a change to become informational, it must be distinguishable in some way.

A difference that cannot be distinguished, preserved, recognized or related to anything else does not function as information within the model.

### Working formulation

Distinguishable change is a transformation that can be recognized as different from another state.

## 3. Information

In PRM, **information** is distinguishable relational difference capable of being recognized, preserved, transmitted or transformed.

Information is not limited to written symbols, digital data or conscious knowledge. It may appear as:

- a signal,
- a pattern,
- a state change,
- a structural difference,
- a relation between states,
- a measurable variation,
- a preserved trace of transformation.

### Working formulation

Information is distinguishable change within a relational field.

## 4. Signal

A **signal** is a propagated form of distinguishable change.

A signal does not have to be interpreted only as an electrical, optical or electromagnetic event. In PRM, a signal is any structured change that can carry a distinguishable difference through a relational medium.

Examples may include:

- electrical signal,
- radio wave,
- light pulse,
- network packet,
- sound wave,
- symbolic message,
- biological impulse,
- state transition in a system.

### Working formulation

A signal is a structured propagation of distinguishable change.

## 5. Medium

A **medium** is the relational structure through which distinguishable change can propagate.

In classical communication, the medium may be cable, air, fiber, radio channel or physical space. In PRM, the medium is understood more generally as the structure that allows change to be carried, constrained, transformed or interpreted.

A medium does not merely “contain” information. It shapes how distinguishable change can move and appear.

### Working formulation

A medium is a relational structure that enables or constrains the propagation of distinguishable change.

## 6. Transmission

**Transmission** is the movement or propagation of distinguishable change across a relational structure.

In PRM, transmission is not only the sending of data. It is the preservation of a difference through transformation.

A transmitted signal may change form, but it remains informational if the relevant distinction is preserved.

For example:

- electrical modulation may become radio emission,
- radio emission may become electrical signal again,
- light pulses may become digital data,
- spoken sound may become meaning in a listener,
- written symbols may become conceptual understanding.

In every case, what matters is not that the physical form remains identical, but that a distinguishable relational difference is preserved through transformation.

### Working formulation

Transmission is the preservation and propagation of distinguishable change across relational transformations.

## 7. Transformation

**Transformation** is a change of form, state or relational organization.

Information may survive transformation if the relevant distinction remains recoverable or recognizable.

This is important because communication often involves many transformations.

A digital message may pass through:

1. symbolic encoding,
2. electrical representation,
3. modulation,
4. physical propagation,
5. reception,
6. decoding,
7. interpretation.

At each stage, the form may change. What persists is a structured difference.

### Working formulation

Transformation is the change of relational form while preserving, modifying or losing distinguishable difference.

## 8. Recognition

Information requires recognition.

Recognition does not always mean conscious understanding. It may mean that a system can respond differently to different states.

A sensor, router, organism, human mind or AI model may all recognize information in different ways.

In PRM, recognition means that a relational structure can distinguish one state from another and react accordingly.

### Working formulation

Recognition is the ability of a relational structure to distinguish and respond to difference.

## 9. Noise

**Noise** is relational disturbance that weakens, distorts or hides distinguishable change.

Noise does not necessarily destroy information completely. It may reduce stability, clarity or recoverability.

From a PRM perspective, noise interferes with the projection of a clear relational distinction.

### Working formulation

Noise is disturbance that reduces the stability or recognizability of distinguishable change.

## 10. Information Loss

**Information loss** occurs when a distinguishable difference can no longer be recovered, recognized or related to its prior structure.

Information may be lost through:

- distortion,
- excessive noise,
- broken relation,
- insufficient resolution,
- failed recognition,
- irreversible transformation,
- missing context.

In PRM terms, information loss is the collapse or disappearance of a distinguishable relational difference.

### Working formulation

Information loss is the disappearance of recoverable relational distinction.

## 11. Communication

Communication is the organized transmission of distinguishable change between relational structures.

A communication process does not require that the same physical form persists from beginning to end. It requires that a relevant distinction is preserved well enough to be recognized by another structure.

### Working formulation

Communication is the relational transfer of distinguishable change.

## 12. Relation to PRM Concepts

Information and change connect directly with the core PRM vocabulary.

### Distinction

Information begins with difference.

### Relation

Information becomes meaningful only through relation to other states, structures or contexts.

### Projection

Information appears as a projection of underlying relational difference.

### Resistance

Stable information requires resistance against distortion or loss.

### Change

Information depends on distinguishable transformation.

### WR

A WR may generate, preserve, receive, transform or interpret information.

## 13. Example: Digital Communication

A digital message sent through a network may appear at different levels:

- text written by a human,
- binary encoding,
- electrical signals,
- optical pulses,
- packet structures,
- routing decisions,
- decoded text,
- interpreted meaning.

Each level uses a different form.

From a PRM perspective, these are not separate realities. They are different projections of distinguishable relational change.

The message exists as long as the relevant distinction is preserved across transformations.

## 14. Example: Radio and Light

Radio transmission and fiber-optic communication may seem technically different.

Radio uses electromagnetic propagation through space.  
Fiber optics uses light guided through optical fiber.

However, at a deeper conceptual level, both can be interpreted as propagation of distinguishable change.

The technical mechanisms are different, but the PRM pattern is similar:

1. a distinction is encoded,
2. the distinction becomes a signal,
3. the signal propagates through a medium,
4. the signal is received,
5. the distinction is reconstructed or interpreted.

## 15. Example: Reasoning

Reasoning also involves information and change.

A question introduces a relational structure.  
Context and sources provide additional distinctions.  
Reasoning transforms these relations.  
The final answer is a projection of that transformation.

If the reasoning preserves the right distinctions, the answer may be stable.  
If it loses or distorts them, the answer may become misleading.

This connects information and change directly with the Reasoning Reviewer Gate.

## Important Limitation

This document does not replace information theory, communication theory or physics.

The purpose is conceptual.

PRM uses the idea of distinguishable change as a common language for thinking about information across different domains.

Any formal or scientific application would require further mathematical and empirical development.

## Summary

In PRM:

- change is transformation in relational structure,
- distinguishable change becomes informational,
- information is relational difference that can be recognized, preserved, transmitted or transformed,
- a signal is propagated distinguishable change,
- a medium enables or constrains propagation,
- communication is transfer of distinguishable change,
- noise weakens recognizability,
- information loss occurs when distinction can no longer be recovered.

The central idea is:

> Information is not only something that is stored or sent.  
> Information is distinguishable change within a relational field.
