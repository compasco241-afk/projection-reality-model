# Communication Model in PRM

**Projection Reality Model — PRM**  
**Communication as propagation of distinguishable change**

## Purpose

This document describes how the Projection Reality Model interprets communication.

In PRM, communication is not treated only as the transfer of messages between separate systems. It is interpreted more generally as the propagation, preservation and reconstruction of distinguishable change across relational structures.

The central idea is:

> Communication occurs when distinguishable change is transferred through a medium and becomes recognizable by another structure.

## General View

Communication can be described technically in many different ways.

Radio communication, fiber-optic transmission, electrical signaling, sound, biological signaling and digital networking all use different mechanisms.

However, at a deeper conceptual level, PRM interprets them through a shared pattern:

1. a distinction is created,
2. the distinction is encoded,
3. the distinction becomes a signal,
4. the signal propagates through a medium,
5. the signal is received,
6. the distinction is reconstructed,
7. the reconstructed distinction becomes information for another structure.

The physical form may change many times, but the communication process continues as long as the relevant distinction is preserved.

## 1. Distinction as the Beginning of Communication

Communication begins with distinction.

Before anything can be communicated, there must be a difference that can be identified.

Examples:

- voltage high versus voltage low,
- light pulse versus no pulse,
- carrier modulation versus no modulation,
- packet present versus packet absent,
- sound pattern versus silence,
- symbol A versus symbol B,
- state change versus no state change.

Without distinction, there is no signal.  
Without signal, there is no communication.

### Working formulation

Communication begins when a distinguishable difference is formed.

## 2. Signal

A signal is a structured carrier of distinguishable change.

In technical systems, a signal may appear as:

- electrical voltage,
- optical pulse,
- electromagnetic wave,
- acoustic wave,
- digital packet,
- modulation pattern,
- symbolic sequence.

In PRM, the signal is not defined only by its physical carrier. It is defined by its ability to carry distinguishable relational difference.

### Working formulation

A signal is a structured propagation of distinguishable change.

## 3. Medium

A medium is the relational structure that allows a signal to propagate.

Different communication systems use different media:

- copper cable,
- optical fiber,
- air,
- vacuum,
- radio channel,
- biological tissue,
- digital network,
- symbolic language,
- computational memory.

The medium does not merely transport information passively. It shapes, limits, distorts, stabilizes or transforms the signal.

### Working formulation

A medium is a relational structure that enables and constrains signal propagation.

## 4. Encoding

Encoding is the transformation of distinction into a signal form that can propagate through a chosen medium.

Examples:

- text encoded as binary data,
- binary data encoded as electrical levels,
- electrical variation encoded as radio modulation,
- packets encoded as optical pulses,
- thought encoded as spoken language,
- sound encoded as digital samples.

Encoding does not preserve the original form directly. It preserves the relevant distinction in a new relational form.

### Working formulation

Encoding is the transformation of distinguishable difference into a propagable signal structure.

## 5. Propagation

Propagation is the movement or continuation of a signal through a medium.

In PRM, propagation means that distinguishable change is carried across relational structure.

The signal may weaken, reflect, scatter, distort or transform, but communication remains possible if the relevant distinction remains recoverable.

### Working formulation

Propagation is the continuation of distinguishable change through a relational medium.

## 6. Reception

Reception is the process by which a propagated signal becomes available to another structure.

Reception does not yet guarantee understanding. It only means that the signal has reached a structure capable of detecting or processing it.

Examples:

- antenna receives radio wave,
- photodiode receives light pulse,
- network interface receives packet,
- ear receives sound wave,
- sensor receives state change,
- AI model receives input tokens.

### Working formulation

Reception is the capture of propagated distinguishable change by another relational structure.

## 7. Decoding

Decoding is the reconstruction of the relevant distinction from the received signal.

The received signal may not be identical to the original signal. It may contain noise, distortion or loss.

Decoding succeeds when the receiving structure can recover the relevant difference well enough for interpretation or further processing.

### Working formulation

Decoding is the reconstruction of distinguishable difference from a received signal.

## 8. Recognition

Recognition occurs when the receiving structure can distinguish the signal as meaningful or operational.

Recognition does not always mean conscious understanding. A router, sensor, organism, machine or human mind may recognize information in different ways.

A router recognizes packet structure.  
A receiver recognizes modulation.  
A person recognizes words.  
An AI model recognizes token patterns.

### Working formulation

Recognition is the ability of a receiving structure to distinguish and respond to received difference.

## 9. Noise and Distortion

Noise is disturbance that weakens or hides distinguishable change.

Distortion is transformation that changes the structure of the signal in a way that may damage recognition.

In PRM, both noise and distortion affect the stability of communication.

Communication fails when the relevant distinction can no longer be recovered.

### Working formulation

Noise and distortion reduce the stability and recoverability of distinguishable change.

## 10. Communication Across Different Technical Systems

Different communication technologies may appear unrelated at the technical level, but PRM interprets them through the same relational pattern.

### Radio

Radio communication uses electromagnetic waves propagating through space.

The message is encoded into modulation.  
The modulation changes the carrier wave.  
The wave propagates through a medium.  
The receiver reconstructs the modulation.  
The original distinction is recovered as information.

### Fiber Optics

Fiber-optic communication uses light guided through optical fiber.

The message is encoded into light pulses or modulation.  
The optical signal propagates through fiber.  
The receiver detects optical changes.  
The distinction is reconstructed as digital information.

### Copper Cable

Electrical communication through copper uses voltage, current or electromagnetic effects along conductors.

The signal propagates through a physical structure.  
The receiver detects changes.  
The relevant distinction is reconstructed.

### Sound

Speech uses air pressure variations.

A thought or intention becomes language.  
Language becomes sound waves.  
Sound propagates through air.  
The ear and brain reconstruct the distinction as meaning.

### Digital Networks

A digital network uses many layers of transformation.

A message may move through:

1. application data,
2. binary encoding,
3. packet structure,
4. electrical or optical signaling,
5. routing,
6. reception,
7. decoding,
8. interpretation.

The message remains communicative only if the relevant distinctions survive these transformations.

## 11. The PRM Communication Pattern

Across all these systems, the same abstract pattern appears:

1. distinction,
2. encoding,
3. signal,
4. medium,
5. propagation,
6. reception,
7. decoding,
8. recognition.

This pattern allows PRM to interpret communication as a relational process rather than as a single physical mechanism.

## 12. Communication and Projection

In PRM, the received message is a projection of the underlying communication process.

The receiver does not access the full history of transformations directly. It receives a projected result: a reconstructed signal, symbol, packet, sound or meaning.

Communication therefore involves projection at every stage.

The final received information is not the original structure itself, but a stabilized reconstruction of distinguishable change.

### Working formulation

A received message is a projection of preserved distinguishable change.

## 13. Communication and Resistance

For communication to work, the signal must resist loss, noise and distortion.

This does not mean the signal remains unchanged. It means that the relevant distinction remains stable enough to be recovered.

In PRM terms, reliable communication requires a form of relational resistance.

Examples:

- error correction,
- redundancy,
- modulation robustness,
- shielding,
- signal amplification,
- timing synchronization,
- protocol structure,
- packet checksums.

All of these increase the stability of distinguishable change.

## 14. Communication and Time

Communication also depends on time.

Signals propagate in sequence.  
Packets arrive in order or out of order.  
Meaning may depend on timing.  
Synchronization may be required.  
Delay may affect interpretation.

In PRM, time appears here as the ordering of communicative change.

### Working formulation

Communication requires the temporal ordering of distinguishable change.

## 15. Communication and Meaning

Meaning appears when received information is related to a context.

A signal alone is not always meaningful. It becomes meaningful when a receiving structure can relate it to a system of interpretation.

For example:

- a voltage change means something only inside an electrical protocol,
- a packet means something inside a network stack,
- a word means something inside a language,
- a measurement means something inside a model,
- an answer means something inside a question-context relation.

In PRM, meaning is not isolated from relation.  
Meaning appears when distinguishable change is placed within a relational context.

### Working formulation

Meaning is recognized relational difference within a context.

## 16. Example: Internet Transmission

When a user sends a message through the Internet, the process may involve many projections:

1. intention,
2. written text,
3. character encoding,
4. binary representation,
5. packets,
6. electrical or optical signals,
7. routing through networks,
8. reception,
9. decoding,
10. displayed text,
11. interpretation by another user.

At every stage, the form changes.  
The communication succeeds if the relevant distinction survives.

From a PRM perspective, the Internet is a large relational system for preserving and propagating distinguishable change.

## 17. Relation to Information and Change

This communication model extends the PRM interpretation of information.

Information is distinguishable change.  
Communication is the transfer of distinguishable change.  
A signal is propagated distinguishable change.  
A medium is the structure through which change propagates.  
Recognition is the recovery of distinction by another structure.

## Important Limitation

This document does not replace telecommunications theory, signal theory, information theory or physics.

It does not claim that all technical differences are unimportant.

Radio, fiber optics, copper, sound and digital networks have different physical and engineering mechanisms.

PRM only proposes a shared conceptual layer:

> communication can be interpreted as propagation of distinguishable relational change.

## Summary

In PRM:

- communication begins with distinction,
- a signal carries distinguishable change,
- a medium enables and constrains propagation,
- encoding transforms distinction into signal form,
- decoding reconstructs distinction,
- recognition makes received difference operational or meaningful,
- noise and distortion weaken recoverability,
- communication succeeds when relevant distinction survives transformation.

The central idea is:

> Communication is the propagation and reconstruction of distinguishable change across relational structures.
