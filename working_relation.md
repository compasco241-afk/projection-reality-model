# Working Relation

**Projection Reality Model — PRM**  
**WR as a stable relational unit within the Projection Reality Model**

## Purpose

This document defines the concept of **WR — Working Relation**.

WR is one of the central working concepts of the Projection Reality Model. It is used to describe a stable relational configuration that can preserve identity, resist change, participate in transformation and project into observable form.

The term is intentionally provisional. It is not meant to prematurely identify the structure as a physical object, mental object, mathematical object or metaphysical entity.

WR is a working unit for analysis.

## Basic Definition

A **Working Relation** is a stable configuration of relations treated as a unit within PRM.

A WR is not defined primarily by material substance.  
It is defined by relational organization.

A WR can be understood as something that:

- contains distinguishable structure,
- preserves some degree of identity,
- maintains internal organization,
- relates to other structures,
- resists disruption,
- participates in change,
- can project into observable form.

### Working formulation

A WR is a stable relational configuration that can be treated as a unit of analysis.

## Why WR Is Needed

PRM describes reality as a structured field of distinguishable relations.

However, to analyze such a field, the model needs a working unit.

WR provides this unit.

Without WR, the model would only speak about relations in general.  
With WR, the model can describe stable structures, transformations, interactions and projections.

WR allows PRM to ask:

- What remains stable?
- What changes?
- What resists change?
- What can interact?
- What can be recognized?
- What can be projected as an object, state or process?

## WR Is Not Necessarily a Physical Object

A WR does not have to be a physical object in the ordinary sense.

A WR may describe:

- a physical structure,
- an informational structure,
- a communication process,
- a reasoning structure,
- a stable pattern,
- a system state,
- a conceptual unit,
- a relation between states.

This does not mean that all these things are the same.  
It means that PRM can analyze them through a shared relational lens.

## WR and Identity

A WR preserves identity by maintaining enough relational organization across change.

Identity does not mean total immobility.

A WR may change internally and still remain identifiable if its core organization persists.

For example:

- a signal may change form but preserve a message,
- a living organism may change matter but preserve organization,
- a concept may be rephrased but preserve meaning,
- a reasoning chain may be revised but preserve its conclusion,
- a system may update state while preserving continuity.

### Working formulation

Identity is the continued recognizability of a WR across transformation.

## WR and Resistance

A WR resists change to some degree.

Resistance is what allows a WR to persist long enough to be recognized.

If a relational structure had no resistance at all, it would dissolve immediately into change.  
If it had absolute resistance, it could not transform.

Most meaningful structures exist between these extremes.

### Working formulation

Resistance is the degree to which a WR preserves its organization against disruption.

## WR and Change

A WR can participate in change.

Change may affect:

- internal structure,
- relation to other WRs,
- observable projection,
- stability,
- informational content,
- capacity for further transformation.

A WR is therefore not a static object.  
It is a relational structure capable of persistence and transformation.

### Working formulation

Change is a distinguishable transformation of a WR or its relations.

## WR and Projection

A WR may project into observable form.

The observed object, signal, state or answer may not reveal the full internal relational structure of the WR.

It may show only a stabilized appearance.

This is why PRM uses the term projection.

A projection is not unreal.  
It is the accessible expression of a deeper relational organization.

### Working formulation

Projection is the observable expression of a WR within a given context.

## WR and Information

A WR may generate, preserve, receive, transform or lose information.

Information appears when a distinguishable change within or between WRs can be recognized.

A WR can therefore be treated as an informational structure when its relational changes carry distinguishable difference.

Examples:

- a router preserving packet distinctions,
- a signal carrying modulation,
- a memory storing state,
- a text preserving meaning,
- a reasoning process preserving premises and conclusions.

## WR and Communication

Communication can be understood as the transfer of distinguishable change between WRs.

One WR produces or modifies a signal.  
Another WR receives and reconstructs the relevant distinction.

In this interpretation, communication is not merely the movement of material substance.  
It is the propagation of distinguishable relational change.

## WR and Reasoning

Reasoning can also be treated as a WR.

A reasoning structure contains:

- a question,
- assumptions,
- definitions,
- sources,
- intermediate steps,
- conclusions,
- final response.

If these relations are stable and coherent, the answer may be reliable.  
If they are weak or distorted, the answer may contain projection errors.

This connects WR directly with the Reasoning Reviewer Gate.

## WR and Protomass

In PRM, **protomass** may be interpreted as stable relational resistance within a WR.

A WR with high protomass strongly preserves its organization.

This does not mean physical mass directly.  
It means that the WR behaves as a stable unit within the conceptual model.

### Working formulation

Protomass is the stable resistance of a WR before physical interpretation.

## WR and Protoenergy

In PRM, **protoenergy** may be interpreted as the capacity of a WR to participate in transformation.

A WR with high protoenergy has strong potential for change, influence or reorganization.

This does not mean physical energy directly.  
It means transformative capacity inside the PRM framework.

### Working formulation

Protoenergy is the capacity of a WR to enter, produce or support change.

## WR and Time

Time may be interpreted as the ordering of possible and actual changes of WR structures.

A WR can be analyzed through:

- previous state,
- current state,
- possible future state,
- transformation path,
- resistance to transition,
- capacity for transition.

In this sense, time is not merely an external background.  
It is also a relational ordering of WR transformation.

## WR and Boundaries

A WR requires some kind of boundary.

This boundary does not have to be a physical wall.  
It may be structural, informational, functional, conceptual or operational.

A boundary allows the WR to be distinguished from what it is not.

Examples:

- the boundary of an object,
- the boundary of a signal,
- the boundary of a process,
- the boundary of a concept,
- the boundary of a reasoning chain,
- the boundary of a system state.

### Working formulation

A WR boundary is the condition that allows a relational configuration to be distinguished as a unit.

## WR and Context

A WR is always interpreted within a context.

The same relational structure may appear differently depending on the observational or operational frame.

For example:

- a network packet may be seen as electrical signal, protocol unit or user data,
- a sentence may be seen as symbols, grammar, meaning or intention,
- a physical object may be seen as matter, structure, function or projection,
- a reasoning answer may be seen as text, conclusion, argument or unstable projection.

Context determines which projection of the WR becomes relevant.

## What WR Is Not

WR is not currently:

- a confirmed physical particle,
- a mathematical object with a complete formal definition,
- a metaphysical substance,
- a replacement for existing scientific entities,
- a claim that all things are identical.

WR is a conceptual unit used to organize analysis inside PRM.

## Open Problems

The WR concept still requires further development.

Open questions include:

1. Can WR be formalized?
2. Can WR boundaries be defined precisely?
3. Can relational resistance be measured?
4. Can WR transformations be modeled?
5. Can WR interactions be described systematically?
6. Can WR apply across physics, information, communication and reasoning without becoming too broad?
7. What would count as a failure of the WR concept?

## Summary

A Working Relation is a stable relational configuration treated as a unit of analysis.

In PRM, WR helps describe:

- identity,
- stability,
- resistance,
- change,
- projection,
- information,
- communication,
- reasoning,
- protomass,
- protoenergy,
- temporal ordering.

The central idea is:

> A WR is not primarily a thing made of substance, but a stable organization of relations capable of persistence, transformation and projection.
